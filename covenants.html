Human Value Governance™

The Seven Covenants

These are not aspirations. They are governance commitments — formal declarations of what an organization will protect, will not delegate, and will be held accountable for preserving in every AI deployment it sanctions.

On the nature of a covenant

A covenant is stronger than a policy and more durable than a principle. It is a mutual commitment between an organization and those it serves — a declaration that certain values will not be traded for convenience, efficiency, or competitive advantage. Human Value Governance™ adopts the covenant as its governing form because the capacities it protects are not negotiable. Each of the seven covenants below names a protected human asset, articulates the commitment required to preserve it, and defines the organizational accountability that makes the commitment real.

Covenant I Protected Asset: Empathy

The Covenant of Empathy

We commit that no AI system deployed by this organization will be positioned to replace human empathic presence in any interaction where that presence is consequential to the wellbeing, dignity, or decision-making of another person. AI may support, augment, or prepare human empathic engagement — it may not substitute for it.

Where empathy is required — in care, counsel, grievance, evaluation, and any context of consequence — a human being who is qualified, present, and accountable shall be engaged. The simulation of empathy by an AI system is not a governance-compliant substitute.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will identify all interaction contexts in which empathic presence is consequential, designate human accountability for those contexts, and prohibit the use of AI as a replacement for human contact in those designated areas.

Covenant II Protected Asset: Moral Judgment

The Covenant of Moral Judgment

We commit that no consequential moral decision — one that materially affects the rights, opportunities, welfare, or dignity of a person — will be delegated to an AI system. Human moral judgment, exercised by a qualified person who bears accountability for the outcome, is required wherever ethical reasoning is at stake.

AI may present options, surface relevant considerations, or model potential consequences. It may not render moral verdicts, recommend ethical conclusions as directives, or be used as cover for decisions that should be owned by a human being.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will establish clear criteria for what constitutes a consequential moral decision, maintain a named human accountable for every such decision, and prohibit AI-generated recommendations from serving as final determinations in those contexts.

Covenant III Protected Asset: Trust

The Covenant of Trust

We commit that this organization will not deploy AI in ways that simulate human trust relationships — that present AI interactions as human, obscure the nature of AI involvement in consequential decisions, or exploit the cognitive and emotional conditions under which human trust is formed.

Trust between human beings is built through time, vulnerability, and demonstrated accountability. It cannot be manufactured by a system that bears no consequence for its failures. We will not permit AI to occupy the space that trust requires a human to fill.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will implement clear disclosure requirements for AI involvement in customer, employee, and stakeholder interactions, and will prohibit AI systems from presenting themselves as human or from obscuring AI involvement in consequential decisions.

Covenant IV Protected Asset: Experiential Wisdom

The Covenant of Experiential Wisdom

We commit that this organization will structure its use of AI in ways that actively preserve and develop the experiential wisdom of its people — that do not shortcut the processes through which professional judgment, institutional knowledge, and hard-won expertise are acquired.

Experiential wisdom is irreproducible by training data. It is accumulated through consequence, failure, and the slow accumulation of embodied professional judgment. We will not deploy AI in ways that systematically deprive our people of the experiences through which wisdom is formed.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will conduct regular assessments of AI's impact on professional development pathways, ensure that AI assistance does not routinely bypass learning experiences essential to developing expert judgment, and preserve mentorship structures that transmit experiential wisdom.

Covenant V Protected Asset: Emotional Presence

The Covenant of Emotional Presence

We commit that this organization will not substitute AI for genuine human emotional presence in contexts where the experience of being truly witnessed, accompanied, and responded to by another person is what the situation requires.

Emotional presence is not a service. It is the experience of a human being choosing to be fully present with another. No AI system can make that choice, bear the weight of that presence, or provide what another human being needs in moments of genuine consequence. We will not pretend otherwise.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will identify contexts in which emotional presence is materially required — grief, crisis, evaluation, significant life or career decisions — and ensure that human beings, not AI systems, bear responsibility for those interactions.

Covenant VI Protected Asset: Critical Thinking

The Covenant of Critical Thinking

We commit that this organization will deploy AI in ways that strengthen, not atrophy, the critical thinking capacity of its people. We recognize that sustained reliance on AI-generated analysis, synthesis, and recommendation produces measurable degradation in independent reasoning — and we commit to governing AI use in a manner that protects against it.

AI may augment human analysis. It may not become the unexamined source of organizational conclusions. Wherever consequential decisions are made, independent human evaluation of AI outputs is a governance requirement, not an optional safeguard.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will establish protocols requiring independent human evaluation of AI outputs in all consequential decisions, measure and report on the critical thinking health of their workforce, and build AI literacy programs that cultivate evaluative skill rather than passive dependency.

Covenant VII The Governing Covenant

The Covenant of Human Primacy

We commit that in every AI deployment this organization sanctions, human beings remain the primary agents — the decision-makers, the accountable parties, the bearers of consequence, and the ultimate sources of the values that govern outcomes. AI is always an instrument. It is never a principal.

This is the governing covenant — the one that encompasses and underwrites all others. Whatever efficiency is gained, whatever capability is unlocked, whatever scale is achieved through AI deployment, none of it justifies the displacement of the human being from the center of her own governance, her own judgment, and her own accountability.

Implementation Commitment

Organizations adopting this covenant will establish a Human Value Governance function — accountable to leadership — with the mandate and authority to review AI deployments for compliance with all seven covenants, report findings to the board, and enforce remediation where covenant commitments are found to be violated.
